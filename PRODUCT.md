# Handy Services (handyservices.app)

register: brand (customer/contractor-facing pages); product (admin dashboard)

## Product purpose
Nottingham home-services company. Demand engine (marketing + AI quoting) + delivery OS
(vetted contractor pool). Two audiences: homeowners/landlords who want jobs handled, and
tradespeople we recruit to deliver.

## Users
- **Customers**: homeowners, landlords, property managers. Judged us against "normal
  handyman who doesn't turn up". Positioning: excellent at everything a normal handyman
  does badly (turn up, communicate, finish, clean up).
- **Contractors** (recruiting surfaces): self-employed UK tradesmen, phone-first, often
  reading in the van or on the sofa via Facebook/WhatsApp links. Money-first, allergic to
  jargon and anything that smells like a pyramid scheme or an agency skim. Autonomy is
  identity: they are their own boss.

## Brand voice
Bold, direct, concrete. Money and promises in plain numbers. No corporate softeners.
Three brand-voice words: **solid, straight-talking, grafting**.

## Visual identity (committed, do not re-litigate)
- Poppins everywhere; weight contrast for hierarchy.
- Slate-900 dark surfaces + amber-400/500 accent (money/CTA color); light body sections
  slate-50/white. PDF/brand swatch: handy.navy #1B2A4A, handy.yellow #F5A623.
- rounded-3xl cards, rounded-full pill CTAs, amber eyebrow headers.
- User's documented taste: **bold high-contrast solid color blocks over subtle tinted
  callouts**; decides fast from concrete variants.

## Anti-references
- Checkatrade/lead-gen platforms (contractors hate them; we are the anti-Checkatrade).
- Aspect Maintenance style commission-mill vibes (deductions, opaque pay).
- Generic SaaS landing template (icon-card grids, gradient text).

## Strategic principles
- Contractor pitch = "sell us your empty days" (surplus-day frame), progression ladder
  to permanent work as second beat. Pay numbers must be real (from live pay engine).
- Customer pitch = reliability per promise; anti-handyman inversion at the moment of doubt.
