Per room, in this order:

  1. Clear the room — move furniture (offer made), cover what stays with dust sheets, seal doorways/fireplace with polythene & tape. Prep is the job — take the time.
  2. Punch every nail head below the surface (Joe, checked by Craig). One missed nail shreds a £40 sanding belt.
  3. Board repairs first — Craig measures damaged/patched boards, sources reclaimed timber to match, fits and secures. Repairs go in BEFORE fine sanding so they blend.
  4. Rough cut — drum sander 24/40 grit, with the grain (diagonal only if boards are cupped). Joe on vacuum & bag changes.
  5. Medium pass — 60/80. Edge sander round the perimeter to match.
  6. Fill — gaps and minor damage with two-part filler mixed with fine sanding dust for colour.
  7. Fine finish — 100/120 drum + edges. Corners by hand/scraper. Craig checks the whole floor on his knees in raking light.
  8. Dust down — full vacuum, tack-cloth wipe, let dust settle, wipe again.
  9. Oil coat 1 — thin and even, with the grain, work to the exit. NO traffic after.
  10. Oil coat 2 — next morning (~12 hrs later), same method. Advise Nicola: light use after 24 hrs, rugs back after a week.

Follow-on job — limestone slabs (same property)

If time permits — concurrent or after the floors

The job: clean Nicola's limestone slabs using specialist diamond-tipped abrasive discs — the right approach for a delicate stone surface. Quoted & paid, ~1 hr for the quoted area (confirm the full extent with her on site). Labour only.

When: runs after the floors, or alongside them ONLY if the slabs are in a different area, well away from any freshly-oiled boards. Grinding dust must never land on wet oil — if there's any doubt, do it after the oil has cured. Craig's call on the day.

Method: work the diamond discs across the stone to cut back staining / etching / old sealer to a clean, even finish. Keep the dust down (wet-work where you can), protect the pointing/grout between the slabs, then rinse and leave clean and dry.

⚠ Kit — sort before the day Confirm with Ben you've got the right diamond discs/pads (grit) and a machine to run them — add it to the Advanced Tool Hire / Screwfix trip. Don't turn up without it.

🧹 Leave it like a showroom Equipment stacked neatly to one side · floors vacuumed · dust sheets folded · walkways clear and safe · oily areas taped off with a polite note · bins bagged and out. Nicola should be able to walk through her house without touching our kit.
