Craig — Your Jobs

You run this job. Joe has his own sheet for the labouring — he works to your instruction. The full detail is on the main job sheet; this is your list.
The Rectory, Church Lane,
Averham NG23 5RB
Customer: Nicola Huthwaite  ·  07738 255001
1
Talk to Nicola first
Before any kit comes off the van, walk the rooms with her and cover: the plan (living room first, then the rest), what to expect (dust, rooms out of use while oil dries), rough timeframe (2 days), and the limestone slab clean that follows. Offer to move furniture if it's not done. Confirm the oil colour before it's opened.
2
Set the room order & brief Joe
Living room first (her request). Tell Joe which room to clear and protect, and get him punching nails while you set up.
3
Board repairs
Measure the damaged and patched boards, source reclaimed timber to match (width, thickness, era), fit and secure. Do this before the fine sanding so the repairs blend in.
4
Machine sanding
You run the drum and edge sanders. Work through the grits: 24/40 (rough) → 60/80 (medium) → 100/120 (fine). With the grain. Joe keeps the vacuum going and changes the bags.
⚠ Check the nails first One proud nail head shreds a belt — make sure Joe's punched them all before you start.
5
Fill the gaps
After the medium pass, fill gaps and minor damage with two-part filler mixed with fine sanding dust for a colour match.
6
Fine finish & check
Final pass at 100/120, corners by hand. Get down on your knees in raking light and check the whole floor before you oil — this is the bit that shows.
7
Oiling — 2 coats
Dust down fully first. Coat 1 thin and even, with the grain, end of the day. Coat 2 the next morning (~12 hrs later). Tell Nicola: light use after 24 hrs, rugs back after a week.
⚠ Don't rush the oil Microfibre roller, not foam. Thin coats. If the last coat won't fit in the 2 days, tell Ben early — a short return beats a rushed finish.
8
Check Joe's work
Before moving on each stage: nails punched, floor vacuumed, protection right, area tidy. You sign it off.
9
Limestone slabs — if time permits
Clean the limestone slabs with the diamond discs — after the floors, or alongside only if they're in a different area. Grinding dust must never land on wet oil. Confirm you've got the right discs + machine before the day.
Your calls to make Room order · oil colour signed off with Nicola before buying · if the 2 days won't cover it, tell Ben by Day 1 lunchtime, not Day 2. Any doubt on the fire/oil safety or the reclaimed timber match — ring Ben.
🔥 Oily rags Any cloth with oil on it — lay it flat outside to dry or drop it in the metal tin. Never balled up in a bag, the van, or Nicola's bin. Make sure Joe does the same.
