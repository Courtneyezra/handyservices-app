The job: Fit worktop · install sink & mixer tap · hang cupboard · tile wall behind counter
Materials: Labour only — Rachel supplies the worktop, sink, tap, cupboard & tiles
⚠ Two things before you start 1. Check everything is on site — worktop, sink, mixer tap, cupboard, tiles, and the tile adhesive/grout. If anything's missing or the wrong size, ring Ben before starting.
2. This is a big job — realistically two days for one person. Agree the plan with Ben and Rachel up front.
1
Hang the wall cupboard
Mark and level, fix solid to the wall with the right fixings for the wall type. Do this first so it's not fighting the worktop later.
⚠ Check first Find pipes and cables before drilling. Cavity fixings on plasterboard, plugs into masonry — it must take the weight of a loaded cupboard.
2
Fit the worktop
Measure, cut and fit the customer-supplied worktop. Scribe to the wall for a clean fit, level it, and fix down securely. Seal the back edge to the wall.
⚠ Measure twice It's Rachel's worktop — one cut. Dry-fit before you commit, and confirm any overhang/cut-out positions with her.
3
Install the sink & mixer tap
Cut the worktop for the sink, fit the sink and the mixer tap, connect the waste and the supply, and seal around the sink. Test for leaks under load.
⚠ Water off first Isolate the supply before disconnecting anything. Check every joint for leaks before you leave — run the tap and fill/drain the sink. If the existing pipework isn't right, stop and call Ben.
4
Tile the wall behind the counter
Set out from centre for an even, balanced finish. Fix tiles with adhesive and spacers, cut neatly around sockets and edges, let them set, then grout and finish with a silicone bead where the tiles meet the worktop.
⚠ Confirm the layout with Rachel Agree tile height and pattern before you stick anything. Leave adhesive to set before grouting — don't rush it into the same hour.
Before you set off Ring Rachel to check she's in and that all her materials have arrived. Confirm with Ben whether tile adhesive, grout and silicone are on us or the customer — labour-only means her materials, but consumables are often missed. Turn up in branded workwear.
