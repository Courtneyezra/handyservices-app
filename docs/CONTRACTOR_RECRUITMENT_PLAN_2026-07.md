## 1. The Offer (why a good tradesperson says yes)

Lead with what they actually want. Do not pitch "join our network" — pitch **selling us
their wasted days**. The ladder (becoming a permanent fixture) is the second beat, not the opener.

**The one-liner:**
> "Keep your business. Sell us your empty days. We fill them with jobs already priced
> and paid — you turn up, do great work, get paid next day. Like it? There's a path to
> guaranteed weekly work when you want more."

**The surplus-day maths (from our last 10 real jobs — see `scripts/_pay-split-last10.ts`):**
- A solo day-rate handyman at the industry-normal 50–70% booking wastes **1½–2½ days a week**.
  A wasted day earns **£0** — it's pure leaked income.
- Our jobs pay **~£194 per booked day** (real average across the last 10 accepted quotes),
  £40–60 minimum per visit, top skilled jobs £50+/hr.
- 2 wasted days/week × £194 ≈ **£1,500+/month of income he's currently binning** — that's
  the number to say out loud. A solo needs **88% of days booked at £220** to match what we
  pay per booked day; almost nobody self-books that full.
- Zero cost to try: no exclusivity, no fees, he keeps every existing client. Worst case he
  declines jobs and nothing changes.

**Why this framing wins (beyond the money):**
- **No identity threat.** He stays his own boss — we're a customer of his business, not a
  new employer. (Research: autonomy is the #1 thing trades value; "join us" reads as
  giving that up, "sell us your dead days" reads as winning.)
- **Legally load-bearing.** Multi-client by construction — his own book stays primary —
  which is the strongest possible self-employment fact pattern (anti-Pimlico). Never ask
  for exclusivity at this tier; it would break both the pitch and the legal position.
- **His surplus days ARE our capacity calendar.** The availability he gives us feeds the
  committed-capacity/residual-book design directly — recruiting and scheduling are the
  same motion.
- **Low-stakes auditioning, both ways.** We vet him on days that cost him nothing; he
  trials us without betting his livelihood. The ladder then converts the proven ones.

**What we hand them (differentiators):**
- Job is already **quoted, scoped, and deposit-paid** — no chasing, no haggling.
- **Paid through the platform** (fast, clean, tax-ready) — not "invoice me and wait."
- **No lead-gen fees / no subscription** (unlike Checkatrade/MyBuilder).
- A **ladder**: ad-hoc (audition) → core (priority routing + committed weekly windows)
  → partner (future equity/city-P&L).

**What we ask of them (the bar):**
- Turn up on time, on-quote, communicate, photo the finish.
- Keep availability current in the app (the "harvester" — this is how they get routed).
- Public-liability insurance + right-to-work + trade references.

## 5. Onboarding funnel (vetting → first job in ≤72h)

Keep it fast but keep the bar. Each new contractor moves through:

1. **Screen call (10 min)** — trades covered, availability, transport, right-to-work,
   insurance. Gut-check reliability. Ben can run these.
2. **Doc collect** — public-liability insurance cert, ID/right-to-work, 1–2 references.
   (Store against the profile.)
3. **Platform setup** — run them through the **contractor onboarding wizard**
   (`ContractorOnboarding.tsx`, <60s → configured provider), set `delivery_tier = adhoc`,
   set day/hourly rate SKUs, get them adding availability in the harvester.
4. **Payout setup** — connect their payout details so they're paid through the platform.
   ⚠️ **Gap: the Stripe payout flow isn't wired yet** — see §7. Until then, log payouts
   manually but still *route the job through the platform* so the data is clean.
5. **Audition job #1** — hand them a small, low-risk, well-scoped job. Craig or Ben
   spot-checks the finish.
6. **Score it** (§2 scorecard). Clean → keep feeding. 5 clean → core review.

**Onboarding checklist (per contractor):**
- [ ] Screen call passed
- [ ] Insurance cert on file
- [ ] Right-to-work + references
- [ ] Profile created, `delivery_tier=adhoc`, rates set
- [ ] Availability entered in app
- [ ] Payout details captured
- [ ] Audition job #1 assigned
- [ ] Job #1 scored
