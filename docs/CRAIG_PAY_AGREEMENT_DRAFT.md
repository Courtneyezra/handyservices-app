# Contractor Services Agreement — DRAFT

**⚠️ Working draft for internal review. Fill the `[bracketed]` fields, then have an accountant/solicitor review before signing — especially the CIS registration and VAT points (§9, §12). This is a template, not legal advice.**

---

**This Agreement** is made between:

- **HandyServices** ("the Company"), [registered address], and
- **Craig [Surname]** ("the Contractor"), trading as [Craig's trading name], [address].

**Commencement date:** [DD/MM/2026]

## 1. Nature of the engagement

1.1 The Contractor is an **independent, self-employed business** providing services to the Company as one of its clients. This Agreement does **not** create a contract of employment or a worker relationship, and does not oblige either party to offer or accept any particular work.

1.2 The Contractor is responsible for their own Income Tax, National Insurance, and (where applicable) VAT, and will account to HMRC directly. _[If the work falls under the Construction Industry Scheme, the Company will register and deduct under CIS — confirm with accountant, see §9.]_

1.3 The Contractor is free to work for other clients and is not required to work exclusively for the Company.

## 2. Right of substitution

2.1 The Contractor may send a suitably qualified and insured substitute to perform any accepted job, provided the substitute meets the quality standard in §6. This is a genuine right, exercisable without the Company's prior approval save for a reasonable competence/insurance check on the substitute.

_(This clause is load-bearing for self-employed status — keep it real and honour it in practice.)_

## 3. Services and how work is offered

3.1 The Company generates and prices jobs. It will **offer** jobs to the Contractor at a **fixed price per job** set by the Company and shown to the Contractor **before** acceptance.

3.2 The Contractor is **free to accept or decline** any job with no consequence to future offers. There is no obligation on the Company to offer, nor on the Contractor to accept, any minimum amount of work (subject to §5).

3.3 The Contractor decides **how** to carry out the work using their own skill and methods. The Company specifies the **outcome and quality standard** (§6), not the working method.

## 4. Payment

4.1 For each completed job, the Contractor is paid the **fixed job price** shown at acceptance. The price is **inclusive of travel and set-up time** — there is no separate on-site-only rate and no unpaid travel. _(Deliberate anti-pattern avoidance: no "time on site only" model.)_

4.2 **No deductions** are made from the job price other than those required by law (e.g. CIS, if applicable). Any dispute over a job price is raised and resolved **before** the job is accepted, not after payment.

4.3 The Contractor **invoices** the Company on completion (per job or on an agreed [weekly/fortnightly] cycle). Payment terms: **[same-day / within X days]** of a satisfactory, photo-verified completion (§7).

4.4 Indicative rates (to be finalised):
- Standard job rate: priced per job by the Company's system, targeting an effective **[£___]–[£___] per hour** to the Contractor after travel.
- Complex / multi-person job (Contractor as lead): fixed job price **plus a lead uplift of [£___] or [__]%** for coordinating and supervising his own crew/substitutes.

## 5. Volume intention (the income floor)

5.1 The Company **intends to offer** the Contractor at least **[£___] of work / [N] jobs per week** while this Agreement is in force, subject to genuine demand.

5.2 This is a statement of the Company's commercial intention to keep the Contractor supplied with work; it is **not** a guarantee of income regardless of work done, and does not create an obligation on the Contractor to accept it. _(Flag for review: a hard income guarantee can create mutuality of obligation and push toward worker status — keep this as an "intention to offer," not a "guaranteed payment.")_

## 6. Quality standard

6.1 The Contractor will perform all work to the **HandyServices Job Standard** (see the Job Standard & Photo-Proof Checklist, appended/linked), which forms part of this Agreement.

6.2 The standard specifies the outcome and finish expected; it does not direct the Contractor's method.

## 7. Photo proof and completion

7.1 For every job, the Contractor submits **before/after photos** and completes the photo-proof checklist. A job is treated as complete (and payable) once photo-verified.

## 8. Defects / rectification

8.1 As an independent business, the Contractor is responsible for putting right any defective work **at their own cost** within [X days] of notification, or the Company may arrange rectification and offset the reasonable cost. _(This risk-bearing is a genuine mark of self-employment.)_

## 9. Tax, CIS and insurance

9.1 The Contractor holds and maintains their own **public liability insurance** (minimum **£[__]m**) and provides evidence on request.

9.2 The Contractor uses **their own tools and equipment**.

9.3 CIS: **[To confirm with accountant]** — where the work is within the Construction Industry Scheme, the Company will verify the Contractor with HMRC and make CIS deductions; otherwise the Contractor accounts for their own tax.

## 10. Materials

10.1 The Company provides job materials via its own trade accounts / a capped company payment card the Contractor may draw on. The Contractor is **not** required to fund materials from their own money. Materials remain the Company's cost and are priced within the job.

## 11. Term and termination

11.1 This Agreement runs from the commencement date until ended by either party on **[X days']** written notice. Either party may end it immediately for material breach.

## 12. Status acknowledgement

12.1 Both parties intend and acknowledge a self-employed, business-to-business relationship. Both understand that **employment status is ultimately determined by the reality of the working relationship, not by this label**, and agree to operate consistently with the terms above (genuine right to decline, substitution, own tools/insurance, control of method, invoicing).

_[VAT note for review: once the Company supplies labour + materials, VAT applies to the whole supply; and the Contractor charges VAT to the Company if VAT-registered. Confirm treatment with accountant before go-live.]_
